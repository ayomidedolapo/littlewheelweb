We clean our homes in spring, but what about our **digital clutter**? From overflowing inboxes to chaotic desktops, our online lives can quietly become a source of stress.

A little digital tidying can go a long way. Here's how to do it—quickly, and without overwhelm.

## Why It Matters

- Cluttered digital spaces make it harder to focus
- You waste time searching for files, links, or info
- A messy digital life creates low-key mental stress

## 1. Inbox Overhaul (10 mins)

Start with your email. You don’t need to reach Inbox Zero, but you _can_ reduce noise.

- Unsubscribe from newsletters you never open
- Archive or delete anything older than 3 months
- Create 2-3 folders: “To Do,” “Read Later,” and “Archive”
- Bonus: Use filters to auto-sort incoming mail

## 2. Desktop Declutter (5 mins)

Your desktop shouldn't be a digital junk drawer.

- Delete screenshots, duplicates, and old downloads
- Create a folder for anything you _might_ need
- Choose a calming wallpaper to reset the vibe

## 3. App & Tab Audit (10 mins)

Too many tabs or unused apps? Time to prune.

- Close all tabs. Reopen only what’s truly important
- Bookmark anything you _actually_ plan to revisit
- Delete apps you haven’t used in 30+ days
- Turn off notifications for non-essential apps

## 4. Password & Security Refresh (15 mins)

It’s not fun, but it’s important.

1. Update your main account passwords
2. Enable two-factor authentication where possible
3. Use a password manager (LastPass, Bitwarden, etc.)
4. Check for old logins via [haveibeenpwned.com](https://haveibeenpwned.com)

## 5. File System Fix-Up (10 mins)

Sort out your cloud or local files. Keep it simple.

- Create folders by year or category (Work, Personal, Projects)
- Delete redundant or outdated files
- Rename vague files like `FinalFINALv3.docx` to something useful

---

### You’re Done!

In under an hour, you’ve:

✅ Reduced mental noise  
✅ Protected your security  
✅ Boosted your daily workflow

Your future self will thank you.
